## 15.2 Optimism in Workplace

> 🧠 **Memory Trick:** **"HIN"** = **H**uman nature → **I**ndividual difference → **N**ot always pessimism

### Optimism as Human Nature
- Early psychologists saw optimism as an illusion; later recognised as a basic human trait

### Optimism as Individual Difference
- People vary in optimism levels based on cognitive expectations and causal attributions
- **Seligman's model:**

```
PESSIMIST attributes bad events as:        OPTIMIST attributes bad events as:
  Internal   (my fault)                      External   (not my fault)
  Stable     (will last long)                Unstable   (temporary)
  Global     (affects everything)            Specific   (only this situation)
```

### Unresolved Issues
- **Little vs. Big optimism** → optimism varies in degree across individuals
- **Optimism vs. Pessimism** → they may NOT be opposites
- **Learning optimism** → optimism can be trained, it's not fixed

### Jobs & Optimism
- High optimism suits: Sales, advertising, health, customer service, PR
- Mild pessimism suits: Accounting, financial control, safety-critical jobs

### Seligman's Met Life Study (Key Numbers to Remember)
- Tool used: **ASQ** (Attributional Style Questionnaire)
- Optimistic agents sold **37% more** in first 2 years vs pessimists
- Top 10% optimists sold **88% more** than the bottom 10% pessimists
- "Special Force" (ASQ optimists who failed industry test) outsold pessimists by **57%** in Year 2

```
Met Life Study Flow:
200 agents (ASQ test)
    → Optimistic half sold 37% more
        → Pilot: 104 new hires (ASQ + industry test)
            → Full study: 15,000 applicants, 1000 hired + 129 Special Force
                → Year 2: Optimists outsold pessimists by 31%
```

## 15.3 Happiness — Objective or Subjective?

> 🧠 **Memory Trick:** **"SWB = SL + A"** → Subjective Well-Being = Satisfaction with Life + Affect

### Objective vs Subjective Debate
- Some say happiness can't be measured objectively (you can fake a smile)
- Daniel Kahneman supports objective measurement via repeated mood assessments
- Currently, **subjective paradigm dominates**

### Formula
```
SWB (Subjective Well-Being) = SATISFACTION WITH LIFE + AFFECT
```

### Why Well-Being Research is Growing
- Western affluence → survival is no longer the focus; quality of life matters
- Rising individualism → personal happiness is more valued
- Better measurement tools available now

### Benefits of Happiness at Work
- Leads to better health, creativity, sociability, self-regulation
- Happy people persist longer, multitask better, and are more attentive
- Well-being is even linked to **longer life (longevity)**

### Theories on Changing Happiness
| Theory | Main Idea |
|---|---|
| Zero-Sum Theory | Happy & unhappy periods alternate; you can't permanently increase happiness |
| Fixed Trait Theory | Happiness is stable; hard to change after early adulthood |
| Adaptation Theory | Happiness returns to baseline after major events (lottery win OR paralysis) |

- Exception: People never fully adapt to **widowhood, loneliness, or injustice**

## 15.4 Spiritual Intelligence (SQ) at Workplace

> 🧠 **Memory Trick:** **"IQ thinks, EQ feels, SQ asks WHY"**

- **IQ** = rational/logical thinking (early 20th century focus)
- **EQ** = emotional intelligence (popularised by Goleman, mid-1990s)
- **SQ** = asks deeper questions about meaning, values, and purpose — the "ultimate intelligence"
- SQ is the **foundation** for both IQ and EQ

### Three Types of Thinking (Zohar & Marshall)
```
Serial Thinking     → IQ → Left brain  → Logical, rational
Associative Thinking → EQ → Right brain → Pattern recognition, social skills
Unitive Thinking    → SQ → Whole brain → Creative, intuitive, inventive
```

### Spirituality vs Spiritual Intelligence
- **Spirituality** = personal search for sacred meaning, transcendence
- **Spiritual Intelligence** = applying those themes practically to improve daily functioning

### Definitions
- **Vaughan (2002):** SQ = deep understanding of existential questions + insight into consciousness levels
- **Wolman (2001):** SQ = capacity to ask life's ultimate questions + feel connected to the world

## 15.5 Concept of SQ

> 🧠 **Memory Trick:** **"3S Brain"** = Serial → IQ, Synchronous → SQ, (Parallel → EQ)

### Three Brain Processing Modes
| Mode | Intelligence | Brain Area |
|---|---|---|
| Serial | IQ | Left brain |
| Parallel | EQ | Right brain |
| Synchronous | SQ | Whole brain |

### Neural Basis
- **40Hz oscillations** across the whole brain = neural basis of SQ
- These link mind, self, and world into a meaningful whole

### SQ Brain Circuitry
- SQ is **hard-wired** in the brain but stays dormant unless actively used
- SQ can be intentionally activated — turning it into a personal resource

### Qualities of SQ
- Wisdom, compassion, integrity, joy, love, creativity, peace
- These come from identifying with **consciousness** (soul) rather than ego/body

## 15.6 Organizational Spirituality

> 🧠 **Memory Trick:** **"BIG JMR RT"** (values framework) = Benevolence, Integrity, Generativity, Justice, Mutuality, Receptivity, Respect, Trust (+ Humanism)

- Jurkiewicz & Giacalone (2004): Workplace spirituality → better motivation, commitment, adaptability
- Spirituality at work = recognising employees have an **inner life** nourished by meaningful work in community
- Three aspects of spirituality at work: **Inner life + Meaningful work + Community**
- Leaders with **higher spirituality scores** led the better-performing units

### Practical Applications of SQ at Work
- Find inner resources to care, tolerate, adapt
- Build stable identity amid changing workplace relationships
- Discern real meaning of work events
- Align personal values with purpose
- Live values without compromise (integrity)
- Understand how the **ego sabotages** all of the above

## 15.7 Contribution of SQ to Managerial Leadership

> 🧠 **Memory Trick:** **"VAH"** = Vision + Altruistic Love + Hope/Faith (SLT's 3 qualities)

### Transcendental Leadership Theory
- Integrates transactional + transformational leadership with spirituality
- Three dimensions: **Consciousness → Moral Character → Faith**
- As leaders grow spiritually: more awareness, universal values, inner direction → better leadership

### Spiritual Leadership Theory (SLT)
```
Vision         → Defines destination, reflects high ideals
Altruistic Love → Care, harmony, well-being for self and others
Hope/Faith      → Perseverance, optimism, do-what-it-takes attitude
```
- Result: Employees feel meaning and calling → higher commitment → higher productivity

### SQ & Employee Engagement
- SQ enables CEOs to use intuition + transcend linear thinking → better holistic decisions
- Spirituality at work creates **community + connectedness** → loyalty + belonging
- Spiritual relationships produce: intimacy, wholeness, authenticity, altruism, integrity

### 5 Perspectives of SQ on Organisations
- a) SQ enhances EQ and Social Intelligence
- b) SQ improves employee quality of life and well-being
- c) High SQ + EQ → sense of purpose and meaning
- d) SQ makes employees feel interconnected and socially responsible
- e) SQ fosters inclusive, evolved behaviour in employees and leaders

### 7 Practical Steps to Better SQ (Zohar & Marshall)
1. Become aware of where I am now
2. Feel strongly what I want to change
3. Reflect on my own centre and deepest motivations
4. Discover and dissolve obstacles
5. Explore many possibilities
6. Commit to a path
7. Remain aware that there are many paths

## 15.8 Emotional Intelligence (EI)

> 🧠 **Memory Trick:** **"PRUE"** = Perceive, Regulate, Understand, Express — the 4 core EI actions

- EI = ability to recognise, understand, and manage your own emotions + relationships with others
- **Salovey & Mayer's definition:** Ability to perceive, access, generate, understand, and regulate emotions to promote intellectual and emotional growth
- Interpersonal skills now proven to be a **greater predictor of success** than technical skills
- EI affects: work performance, physical health, stress management, relationships

---

## 15.9 Models of Emotional Intelligence

> 🧠 **Memory Trick:** **"TAM"** = Trait → Ability → Mixed (3 main EI models)

### 1. Trait Model
- EI as personality-based self-perceptions about recognising and using emotions
- Measured by: EQ-i, SUEIT, SSEIT, Six Seconds EI assessment

### 2. Ability Model (Mayer-Salovey)
- Emotions = vital information sources for navigating social environments
- Four ability branches:
```
1. Perceiving emotions
2. Using emotions (to aid thought)
3. Understanding emotions
4. Managing/regulating emotions
```
- Measurement tool: **MSCEIT** (Mayer-Salovey-Caruso Emotional Intelligence Test)

### 3. Mixed Models

#### Bar-On's Model
- EI = potential for performance (process-oriented, not outcome-oriented)
- 5 components:

| Component | Sub-components |
|---|---|
| Intrapersonal | Self-Regard, Self-Awareness, Assertiveness, Independence, Self-Actualization |
| Interpersonal | Empathy, Social Responsibility, Interpersonal Relationships |
| Adaptability | Reality Testing, Flexibility, Problem Solving |
| Stress Management | Stress Tolerance, Impulse Control |
| General Mood | Optimism, Happiness |

- EI **develops over time** and can be improved through training and therapy

#### Goleman's Mixed Model
- EI = cognitive ability + personality combined
- **5 primary domains:**
```
1. Self-Awareness   → Know your feelings and weaknesses
2. Self-Regulation  → Control impulses; "think before acting"
3. Internal Motivation → Work for internal joy/curiosity, not just rewards
4. Empathy          → Read and respond to others' emotions
5. Social Skill     → Build networks, find common ground
```

#### Mayer & Salovey Four-Branch Model (1997)
```
Branch 1: Emotional Perception  → Accurately read emotions in self/others
Branch 2: Emotional Integration → Use emotions to aid thinking
Branch 3: Emotional Understanding → Know how emotions evolve/blend
Branch 4: Emotional Management  → Regulate emotions for growth
```

## 15.10 Applications of Emotional Intelligence

> 🧠 **Memory Trick:** **"LTC"** = Leadership + Teamwork + Conflict (3 key EI applications)

### EI and Leadership
- High EI leaders consistently outperform peers
- Goleman: EI is more decisive than IQ in leadership roles
- Leaders with high EI: know employee feelings, control own emotions, build confident relationships

### EI and Team Building
- EI improves team dynamics and goal achievement
- EI training helps teams:
  - Assign work more effectively
  - Improve communication
  - Minimise conflict
  - Design better problem-solving groups

### EI and Conflict
- Conflict often stems from EI differences between people
- EI training for conflict includes: overcoming EI differences, emotional dynamics, problem solving, working together

## 15.11 Summary (Key Takeaways)

- Today's workplace is high pressure → SQ helps employees find meaning and reduce stress
- EI influences: talent recruitment, leadership identification, teamwork, morale, productivity, innovation
- IQ + EQ + SQ together = complete intelligent employee
- Optimism, well-being, SQ, and EI are all learnable and developable skills
