## Chapter: Unit 4 — Personality, Values and Attitudes

**Overview:** This unit explains how individual personality, values, and attitudes are formed and how they affect behaviour in organisations. It also covers key theories, models, and the role of socialisation.

---

### 4.1 Introduction

- Personality shapes how a person thinks, feels, and acts at work
- No two people are alike — individual differences drive different behaviours
- Behaviour is **caused, motivated, and goal-oriented** (Lavitt's classification)
- Four basic assumptions about people: individual differences, whole person, motivation, human dignity

---

### 4.2 Models to Understand Human Behaviour

- **Classical view:** People are lazy; need control and economic rewards to perform
- **Human relations view (Likert, McGregor, Bennis):** People are positive; thrive with autonomy and democratic leadership

#### Psychoanalytic Model (Freud)

```
Behaviour is driven by:
┌─────────────────────────────────────────┐
│  ID (pleasure/instinct) — unconscious   │
│  EGO (conscious reality check)          │
│  SUPEREGO (conscience/moral values)     │
└─────────────────────────────────────────┘
Conflict between these three shapes behaviour
```

- **Existential Model:** People shape their own identity; not scientifically based but useful

#### Behaviourist vs Cognitive Models

```
Behaviourist:  S ──► R
               (Stimulus → Response)   [Pavlov, Watson]

Cognitive:     S ──► O ──► R
               (Stimulus → Organism → Response)   [Tolman]
```

- Both agree: learning and environment greatly impact behaviour

---

### 4.3 Implications for the Organisation

- Human behaviour is largely **modifiable** through organisational design, training, and development
- Managers must understand individual differences to make good job-fit decisions

---

### 4.4 Personality

- **Gordon Allport's definition:** Dynamic organisation of psycho-physical systems determining unique adjustment to environment
- Personality = how you affect others + how you see yourself + measurable traits
- Personality integrates perception, learning, values, and attitudes into the "total person"

---

### 4.5 Determinants of Personality

```
Personality is shaped by:

HEREDITY ──────────────────────────────┐
(genes, biology, temperament)          │
                                       ▼
ENVIRONMENT ───────────────────► PERSONALITY
(culture, family, social groups)       ▲
                                       │
SITUATION ─────────────────────────────┘
(context changes which trait shows up)
```

- Heredity = internal; Environment = early conditioning; Situation = context-specific

---

### 4.6 Type and Trait Approaches to Personality

- **Trait Approach (Cattell, 1973):** 16 source traits (e.g., Reserved↔Outgoing, Timid↔Venturesome) — steady but no strong scientific link to performance

- **Locus of Control:**
  - *Internals* — believe they control their own fate
  - *Externals* — believe outside forces control what happens to them

- **Machiavellianism:**
  - High Machs: manipulative, logical, "ends justify means," thrive in loosely structured situations
  - Low Machs: respond better to structured environments

- **Type A vs Type B:**

```
Type A                        Type B
──────────────────────────────────────────
Hard-driving, impatient       Easy-going, sociable
Aggressive, competitive       Non-competitive, laid-back
High output, workaholic       Better at complex, judgement tasks
Poor team player              Good team player
```

### 4.7 Theories of Personality

#### Carl Jung's Theory

- Three assumptions: personality develops over time; people can grow and change; personality is a system of interacting sub-systems
- **Introvert:** quiet, inward, avoids social contact
- **Extrovert:** outgoing, expressive, enjoys people
- Most people are **ambiverts** (in between)

#### Problem-Solving Styles (Jung)

```
Data Collection:  Sensing ◄────────────► Intuition
Decision Making:  Thinking ◄────────────► Feeling

4 Combinations:
1. Sensing + Feeling  → People-focused, orderly decisions
2. Intuitive + Feeling → Creative, people-oriented, lacks detail
3. Sensing + Thinking  → Detail + technically sound, less people focus
4. Intuitive + Thinking → Innovative planner, poor implementer
```

#### Erikson's 8 Life Stages

| Stage | Age | Crisis |
|-------|-----|--------|
| 1 — Infancy | 0–1 | Trust vs Mistrust |
| 2 — Early Childhood | 2–3 | Autonomy vs Shame |
| 3 — Play Age | 4–5 | Initiative vs Guilt |
| 4 — School Age | 6–12 | Industry vs Inferiority |
| 5 — Adolescence | Teens | Identity vs Confusion |
| 6 — Young Adulthood | 20s–30s | Intimacy vs Isolation |
| 7 — Adulthood | 40s–50s | Generativity vs Self-absorption |
| 8 — Later Life | 60s+ | Integrity vs Despair |

#### Sheldon's Body Types

- **Endomorph** (fleshy): friendly, people-oriented, slow to react
- **Mesomorph** (athletic): adventurous, aggressive, competitive
- **Ectomorph** (thin): private, socially inhibited, quick to react

#### Sheehy's Passages Theory (5 Adult Crises)

1. Pulling up Roots (18–22): leaving home, independence
2. The Trying Twenties (20s): opportunity vs fear of commitment
3. The Catch-Thirties (30s): renewing or breaking commitments
4. The Deadline Decade (35–45): re-examining life purpose
5. Renewal or Resignation (mid-40s): stability or regret

#### Argyris's Maturation Theory

- Healthy people move from **immaturity → maturity**
- Immaturity: passive, dependent, shallow interests, short-term view
- Maturity: active, independent, deep interests, long-term view
- Most organisations treat employees like children → keep them immature

### 4.8 Impact of Personality Traits on Organisational Behaviour

- **Big Five Factors:** Adjustment, Sociability, Conscientiousness, Agreeableness, Intellectual Openness

Key traits that matter at work:

- **Risk-Taking:** High risk-takers suit roles like stock trading; low risk-takers suit auditing
- **Self-Monitoring:** High self-monitors adapt behaviour to context; low self-monitors stay consistent always
- **Self-Esteem:** High SE → ambitious, goal-driven, resilient; Low SE → sensitive to stress, sets low goals
- **Locus of Control:** Internals take responsibility; Externals blame fate/others
- **Machiavellianism:** High Machs manipulate well; thrive where bargaining/persuasion is rewarded
- **Goal Orientation:**
  - *Learning goal orientation* → seeks to develop skills, handles failure well
  - *Performance goal orientation* → seeks approval, withdraws on failure
- **Introversion vs Extroversion:** Introverts work better alone quietly; extroverts work better in active social environments
- **Dogmatism:** High dogmatics are rigid, depend on authority, need group structure; Low dogmatics are open-minded
- **Authoritarianism:** Prefers hierarchy and directive leaders; related to low intellectual openness

---

### 4.9 Importance of Personality

- Knowing personality helps in: hiring decisions, job-person fit, transfer/promotion, predicting costly defensive behaviours
- Methods to assess personality: Rating Scales, Experimental Procedures, Questionnaires, Projective Tests (TAT, Rorschach Ink-Blot)

---

### 4.10 Attitudes

- Attitudes = evaluative responses (favourable/unfavourable) to people, objects, situations
- Not visible directly — inferred from behaviour and speech
- Not inborn — learned through experience
- Three ways attitudes form:
  1. Direct experience
  2. Association with similar situations
  3. Learning from others

---

### 4.11 Attitudes and Organisation

```
Three Key Work Attitudes:
┌────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ Job Satisfaction    → How you feel about your job │
│ Job Involvement     → How much you identify with it│
│ Organisational      → Loyalty and commitment to   │
│ Commitment            the organisation            │
└────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
These predict: Productivity, Absenteeism, Turnover
```

- Attitudes can be changed through **persuasive communication**, but change is slow (rooted in values)

---

### 4.12 Values

- Values = deep-seated beliefs about what is good/right/preferable — core of personality
- Values are stable, embedded, and inferred from behaviour

#### Rokeach's Two Value Types

```
Terminal Values         Instrumental Values
(End goals)             (Means to reach goals)
─────────────────────────────────────────────
Comfortable life        Ambition
Family security         Honesty
Self-respect            Courage
Sense of accomplishment Imagination
```

#### Allport's 6 Value Types

| Type | Focus |
|------|-------|
| Theoretical | Truth, rational thinking |
| Economic | Usefulness, practicality |
| Aesthetic | Beauty, form, harmony |
| Social | Love of people |
| Political | Power, influence |
| Religious | Unity and cosmic understanding |

#### Values in Organisations

- Values operate at 3 levels: **Individual → Socio-cultural → Organisational**
- Core values (e.g., NTPC's COMIT: Customer focus, Organisational pride, Mutual respect, Initiative, Total quality) guide employee behaviour
- **Value Actualisation:** Employees must internalise org values, not just know them
- Globalisation challenge: Org values may clash with local cultural values

---

### 4.13 Socialisation's Influence on Personality, Values and Attitudes

- **Socialisation** = process of adapting to an organisation's norms, values, and ways of working

#### Stages of Socialisation

```
Stage 1: Pre-arrival
(Forms expectations before joining)
        │
        ▼
Stage 2: Encounter
(Initial orientation, training, meeting colleagues)
        │
        ▼
Stage 3: Change & Acquisition
(Gradually adopts org's personality, values, attitudes)
```

- Socialisation is **continuous** — happens every time someone moves up the hierarchy

---

### 4.14 Schein Socialisation Model

Three ways employees respond to organisational pressure:

```
┌──────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ 1. REBELLION     → Fights org norms (may get fired)  │
│ 2. CREATIVE      → Accepts key values, rejects       │
│    INDIVIDUALISM   unimportant ones (best balance)   │
│ 3. CONFORM       → Fully accepts all norms (passive) │
└──────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

- Personality, values, and attitudes evolve **continuously** through socialisation
