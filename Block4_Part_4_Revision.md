## Unit 14: Diversity Management

**Overview:** Diversity management is about recognising and using differences among employees (gender, race, age, etc.) to improve organisational performance. Managers must handle diversity carefully — it can be both an opportunity and a challenge.

🧠 **Memory Trick for all topics:** Use the mnemonic **"MEN DID BECOME REALLY WISE"**
- **M**eaning → **N**eed → **D**iversity Levels → **D**iscrimination → **B**iographical Characteristics → **R**ise of Diversity → **W**orkplace Diversity → **I**mplementing Strategy

---

### 14.2 Meaning of Diversity Management

🧠 **Trick:** *"D.M. = Differences Matter"*

- Diversity = addressing multiple lifestyles, personal characteristics within a group
- Covers race, culture, religion, disability, age, sexual orientation, pregnancy, education
- Goes beyond legal compliance → promotes comfort and inclusion inside the organisation
- Social diversity adds richness to the company community and improves quality of life

---

### 14.3 Need for Diversity in Organisation

🧠 **Trick:** *"Non-compliant to Proactive = Liability to Asset"* (bottom to top of the pyramid)

- Key question every org faces: *"Why care about diversity?"*
- Fink, Pastore & Riemer (2003) built a 4-level framework:

```
        /\
       /  \   ← PROACTIVE   (Asset / High positive outcome)
      /----\
     / REACTIVE \
    /------------\
   /  COMPLIANT   \
  /----------------\
 /  NON-COMPLIANT   \  ← LIABILITY / Low positive outcome
/____________________\
```

- Proactive orgs include employees in decisions → gain the most benefit from diversity
- Understanding diversity helps retain good talent and attract new talent

---

### 14.4 Levels of Diversity

🧠 **Trick:** *"Surface = See it. Deep = Feel it."*

- **Surface Level Diversity** — visible differences: age, gender, race, ethnicity, disability
  - Easy to notice → easy to discriminate on (e.g., preferring young hires)
  - Innate — cannot be changed (e.g., race, gender)

- **Deep Level Diversity** — hidden differences: values, beliefs, attitudes, personality
  - Communicated through behaviour (verbal/non-verbal), hard to measure
  - Becomes more important as people interact more over time

```
SURFACE LEVEL          DEEP LEVEL
────────────────       ──────────────────
Visible                Not visible
Age, Gender, Race      Values, Beliefs, Personality
Easy to measure        Hard to measure
Innate / Fixed         Revealed through interaction
```

### 14.5 Discrimination

🧠 **Trick:** *"Unfair D = Same Box, Different People"* (treating everyone in a group as identical)

- Discrimination = letting stereotypes about a group influence your behaviour
- Unfair discrimination ignores individual traits → groups everyone the same
- Harms org: reduces productivity, increases turnover, causes conflict
- Modern discrimination is more covert: exclusion, incivility (L.M. Cortina, 2008)

**India's Key Diversity Concerns (Table 14.1 summary):**

| Diversity Attribute | Key Concern |
|---|---|
| Gender | Remote work, networking for new mothers |
| Age | Mentoring youth; reskilling older workers |
| City vs Hinterland | Language/culture training |
| Geography & Religion | Respect for other cultures |
| Physically Challenged | Fair opportunities & promotion |
| Sexual Orientation | Non-discriminatory policies |

### 14.6 Biographical Characteristics

🧠 **Trick:** *"GRADS" — Gender, Race, Age, Disability, Seniority*

- Biographical characteristics = visible, innate traits (age, gender, race, disability, tenure)
- They seem important but research shows they don't strongly predict job performance

**Age**
- Older workers: experienced, ethical, committed BUT less flexible, tech-resistant
- Age ↑ → Performance ↓ slightly, but Absenteeism ↓ (older workers show up more)
- Age discrimination climate → lower commitment → lower org performance (Kunze et al., 2011)

**Sex/Gender**
- No consistent difference in problem solving, analytics, motivation, or learning
- Women: higher turnover and absenteeism rates than men (research evidence)
- No significant difference in job productivity over the last 40 years

**Disability**
- People with disabilities often get higher performance evaluations
- BUT they face lower expectations and are less likely to be hired (Ren et al., 2008)
- India's Disability Act 2016: expanded coverage from 7 → 21 disabilities; 4% govt job reservation

**Tenure (Seniority)**
- Longer tenure → better productivity, less absenteeism, lower turnover
- Tenure is a more stable predictor of job satisfaction than age alone

**Religion**
- Law prohibits religious discrimination; India's diversity includes all major faiths
- Religious beliefs can affect work behaviour (e.g., prayer times, dietary rules)

**Sexual Orientation & Gender Identity**
- Delhi HC decriminalised homosexuality in 2009 (overturned Section 377)
- Fortune 500 companies with gender identity policies grew from 8 (2001) → 124 (2006)

### Ability (under Biographical Characteristics)

🧠 **Trick:** *"Intellectual = Brain jobs. Physical = Body jobs."*

**Intellectual Ability** — mental tasks: reasoning, problem solving, thinking
- Higher intelligence → higher education → higher pay, leadership potential
- Zero correlation between intelligence and job satisfaction

**Physical Ability** — 9 basic types (Fleishman, 1998):

| Category | Abilities |
|---|---|
| Strength | Dynamic, Trunk, Static, Explosive |
| Flexibility | Extent, Dynamic |
| Other | Body Coordination, Balance, Stamina |

- High score on one physical ability ≠ high score on another

### 14.7 What is Diversity at the Workplace?

🧠 **Trick:** *"Worman's 3 S's → Social, Value (psychology), Info"*

- People differ in: age, gender, culture, education, lifestyle, nationality, skills
- Dianah Worman (2005) split diversity into 3 types:
  - **Social Diversity** — ethics, gender, age
  - **Value Diversity** — personality, attitude differences
  - **Information Diversity** — education, functional tenure
- Diverse employees should NOT be forced into one culture — they should be managed wisely

---

### 14.8 The Reason Behind Rise of Diversity

🧠 **Trick:** *"GMWAP" — Globalisation, Migration, Women, Aging, Politics*

- **Globalisation** → open markets → diverse workforce in skills, culture, age, orientation
- **Migration** → developed nations import workers; developing nations (like India) export workers
- **Women Workers** → more women entering male-dominated professions; policy changes
- **Aging Population** → better healthcare → more seniors working; median work age rising
- **Political Diversity** → different political views in teams → source of conflict on national issues

---

### 14.9 Diversity: Threat or Opportunity?

🧠 **Trick:** *"Ignore diversity = lose customers, employees, and profit"*

- Diversity is unavoidable — no org has a homogenous workforce, customer base, or supply chain
- Ignoring diversity → destroyed relationships, lost customers, policy failures, profit decline
- Non-diversity risk: social gaps, fundamentalism, civil unrest, political confrontations
- Successful businesses capitalise on minorities, women, immigrants, older and disabled workers
- Diversity ≠ just race/ethnicity/gender — it's any quality that makes people different

```
DIVERSITY AS THREAT              DIVERSITY AS OPPORTUNITY
────────────────────             ──────────────────────────
Miscommunication                 Innovation
Conflict                         Better morale
Misunderstanding                 Teamwork
Policy failure                   Wider talent pool
Lost customers                   Better market reach
```

### 14.10 Implementing Diversity Management Strategies

🧠 **Trick:** *"ASCR" — Attract, Select, Career-develop, Retain*

**Attracting Diverse Employees**
- Target recruitment ads at specific demographic groups (universities, minority associations)
- Ads showing diverse employees attract more women and minorities
- Ads with NO diversity signal a poor diversity climate

**Selection**
- Hiring managers must value fairness and objectivity
- Clear non-discrimination policy → qualifications matter more than demographics

**Career Development**
- Personality similarity to co-workers can boost promotion chances
- Collectivistic cultures: similarity to supervisor matters more
- Individualistic cultures: similarity to peers matters more
- Deep-level diversity factors shape reactions more than surface-level traits

**Retaining Employees**
- Demographically different employees feel lower commitment → higher turnover
- Effect weakens as employees get to know each other over time
- Indian study: women and reserved-category employees perceive orgs as less fair and less inclusive

---

### 14.11 Case Study: Shell

- Shell promotes diversity to give all employees equal opportunity to develop skills
- Finance function uses a global steering committee to drive diversity agenda
- Senior leadership support → positive cultural shift toward valuing diversity
- Focus areas: gender diversity + ethnic diversity (especially Asian talent)
- Key insight: "Winning hearts and minds" alone is not enough — need structures, targets, and processes

---

### 14.12 Summary (Key Takeaways)

| Variable | Manager's Action |
|---|---|
| Biographical Characteristics | Don't base decisions on them; watch for implicit bias |
| Selection | Use deep job analysis; match skills to job needs |
| Promotion/Transfer | Consider diversity, org culture, and policies |
| Disability | Modify equipment, infrastructure, and task design |
| Ongoing Diversity | Embed into recruitment, hiring, retention, and development |
| Diversity Climate | Multi-level, comprehensive programmes work best |
