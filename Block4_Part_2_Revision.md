## Unit 12: Organisation Citizenship Behaviour (OCB)

**Overview:** OCB covers the voluntary, extra-role behaviours employees show at work that are not required by their job description but benefit colleagues and the organisation. Encouraging OCB boosts productivity, reduces turnover, and improves customer satisfaction.

---

### 12.1 Introduction

- OCB = anything positive employees do *voluntarily* beyond their formal job duties
- OCB employees are not always top performers, but they always "go the extra mile"
- Benefits of OCB: ↑ productivity, ↑ efficiency, ↑ customer satisfaction, ↓ costs, ↓ turnover, ↓ absenteeism *(Podsakoff et al., 2009)*

---

### 12.2 Concept & Definition

- OCB is a personal choice — but organisations *can* create conditions that encourage it
- OCB has shown to improve organisational effectiveness by **18–38%** *(Podsakoff et al., 2000)*

**Key Definitions (chronological):**

| Scholar | Year | Core Idea |
|---|---|---|
| Barnard | 1938 | Willingness to cooperate beyond self-interest |
| Katz | 1964 | Innovative & spontaneous behaviour, not in job description |
| Organ | 1988 | Discretionary behaviour, not formally rewarded, promotes org effectiveness |
| Van Dyne et al. | 1995 | Extra-role behaviour intended to benefit the organisation |

---

### 12.3 Evolution of the Concept

```
Barnard (1938)         Katz (1964)            Organ (1988)
"Willingness to  -->  "Spontaneous, not  -->  Formal definition
 cooperate"            obligatory acts"        + 5 dimensions
```

- Barnard → first to describe voluntary cooperative effort
- Katz → separated OCB from obligatory role performance
- Organ → named it, defined it, identified 5 categories

---

### 12.4 Forms and Dimensions of OCB

**Two Forms:**

```
OCB
├── OCB-O (Organisation-directed)
│     e.g. organising a campus clean-up drive
└── OCB-I (Individual-directed)
      e.g. helping a colleague finish their task
```

- OCB-I → directly benefits individuals, *indirectly* benefits the org
- OCB-O → directly benefits the organisation

**Five Dimensions (Organ, 1988):**

| Dimension | What it means | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **Altruism** | Helping others | Guiding a new joiner |
| **Conscientiousness** | Going beyond minimum duty | Finishing work before deadline |
| **Sportsmanship** | Tolerating problems, finding solutions | Not complaining; solving instead |
| **Courtesy** | Sharing info to prevent problems | Informing teammates of updates |
| **Civic Virtue** | Supporting org interests | Leading CSR volunteer activities |

> **Measuring tip:** Altruism + Courtesy → **OCB-I**; Conscientiousness + Civic Virtue + Sportsmanship → **OCB-O**

---

### 12.5 Antecedents of OCB (What Causes OCB?)

- **Job Satisfaction** → best single predictor of OCB *(Smith & Bateman, 1983)*
- **Affective Organisational Commitment** → strong belief in org goals → more OCB without expecting rewards
- **Perceived Organisational Support (POS)** → employee feels valued → more OCB
- **Perceived Supervisory Support (PSS)** → supervisor cares about subordinate → more OCB
- **Organisational Justice** → fair treatment → more OCB *(Moorman, 1991)*
- **Group Characteristics** → cohesion, team exchange, group potency → more OCB
- **Leadership** → Transformational, Supportive, Instrumental styles + high LMX → more OCB
- **Personality** → Conscientiousness & Agreeableness positively linked to OCB

```
Key Antecedents → OCB
─────────────────────────────
Job Satisfaction       ──┐
Org Commitment         ──┤
POS / PSS              ──┼──► Higher OCB
Org Justice            ──┤
Leadership (LMX)       ──┤
Personality traits     ──┘
```

---

### 12.6 Implications of OCB

- ↑ Overall organisational performance
- ↑ Employee performance & well-being (via job satisfaction link)
- ↑ Co-worker productivity through group cohesiveness
- Better use of scarce resources
- ↑ Ability to attract & retain top talent
- ↑ Employee cooperation with peers and managers
- ↑ Willingness to sacrifice personal comfort for org goals
- ↓ Turnover and absenteeism
- ↑ Productivity, efficiency, customer satisfaction
- Helps build social capital inside the organisation

---

### 12.7 OCB in Practice (How to Promote It)

> "The extent to which employees exhibit OCB is a function of **ability, motivation, and opportunity**." — Organ et al., 2006

| Strategy | What to Do |
|---|---|
| **Sense of Belongingness** | Office events, get-togethers, cultural programmes |
| **Enlightening Supervisors** | Train supervisors to spot and reward OCB |
| **Recruitment** | Hire outgoing, positive, enthusiastic candidates; use psychometric tests |
| **Environment** | Remove group norms that block initiative |
| **Top Management** | Leaders must model and encourage extra-role behaviour |

---

### 12.8 Measuring OCB

- Most popular tool: **5-Factor Model** *(Organ, 1988)* measured via questionnaire
- Survey items target each of the 5 dimensions

**Sample items:**

| Item | Maps to |
|---|---|
| "I help my colleagues out wherever possible" | OCB-I (Altruism) |
| "I don't mind staying late to finish work even if unpaid" | OCB-O (Sportsmanship) |

- Surveys can be run periodically to monitor OCB trends or test training effectiveness
- If OCB-O is lower than OCB-I → focus improvement efforts on civic virtue, attendance at optional meetings, etc.

---

### 12.9 OCB: Potential Pitfalls

| Pitfall | Risk |
|---|---|
| **Gender Bias** | Women's OCB is less recognised/rewarded than men's *(Heiman & Chen, 2005)* |
| **Organisational Justice** | Unequal rewarding of OCB → perceived unfairness → counterproductive behaviour (theft, absenteeism) |
| **Conditioning** | OCB done too routinely may become an *expected norm*, losing its voluntary nature |

---

### 12.10 Summary (Key Takeaways)

- OCB = voluntary, extra-role, beneficial behaviour — not formally required or rewarded
- Has multiple dimensions (5-factor model) and two broad forms (OCB-I, OCB-O)
- Caused by multiple factors — no single root cause
- Measurable via surveys targeting the 5 dimensions
- Organisations should actively but carefully promote OCB, watching for bias and over-conditioning
